PRO 1:1 The proverbs of Solomon son of David, the king of Israel.
PRO 1:2 These proverbs are to teach wisdom and instruction, to teach words of insight,
PRO 1:3 that you may receive instruction in prudent behavior, righteousness, justice, and uprightness.
PRO 1:4 These proverbs are also to give wisdom to the naive, and to give knowledge and discretion to the youth.
PRO 1:5 Let wise people listen and increase their learning, and let discerning people get wise counsel,
PRO 1:6 to understand proverbs, parables, and words of wise people and their riddles.
PRO 1:7 The fear of Yahweh is the beginning of knowledge— fools despise wisdom and instruction.
PRO 1:8 My son, hear the instruction of your father and do not abandon the rules of your mother;
PRO 1:9 they will be a graceful wreath for your head and pendants hanging from your neck.
PRO 1:10 My son, if sinners try to entice you into their sin, do not go with them.
PRO 1:11 If they say, "Come with us, let us lie in wait for blood, let us hide and attack innocent people for no reason;
PRO 1:12 let us swallow them up alive, like Sheol takes away those who are healthy, and make them like those who go down into the pit;
PRO 1:13 we will find all kinds of valuable things; we will fill our houses with plunder;
PRO 1:14 throw in your lot with us, and we will all have one purse together"—
PRO 1:15 my son, do not walk down that path with them; do not let your foot touch where they walk;
PRO 1:16 their feet run to evil and they hurry to shed blood.
PRO 1:17 For it is useless to spread the net in the sight of any bird.
PRO 1:18 These men lie in wait for their own blood— they set an ambush for their own lives.
PRO 1:19 So are the ways of everyone who gains profit by violence; unjust gain takes away the lives of those who hold on to it.
PRO 1:20 Wisdom cries aloud in the street, she raises her voice in the open places;
PRO 1:21 at the head of the noisy streets she cries out, at the entrance of the city gates she speaks her sayings,
PRO 1:22 "How long, you naive people, will you love being naive? How long, you mockers, will you delight in mockery, and how long, you fools, will you hate knowledge?
PRO 1:23 Turn at my correction; I will pour out my spirit to you; I will make my words known to you.
PRO 1:24 I have called, and you have refused to listen; I reached out with my hand, but there was no one who paid attention.
PRO 1:25 But you have ignored all my instruction and paid no attention to my correction.
PRO 1:26 I will laugh at your calamity, I will mock you when the terror comes—
PRO 1:27 when your fearful dread comes like a storm and disaster sweeps over you like a whirlwind, when distress and anguish come upon you.
PRO 1:28 Then they will call upon me, and I will not answer; they will diligently seek me, but they will not find me.
PRO 1:29 Because they hate knowledge and did not choose the fear of Yahweh,
PRO 1:30 they would not follow my instruction, and they despised all my correction.
PRO 1:31 They will eat the fruit of their ways, and with the fruit of their schemes they will be filled.
PRO 1:32 For the naive are killed when they turn away, and the indifference of fools will destroy them.
PRO 1:33 But whoever listens to me will dwell in safety and will rest secure with no fear of disaster."
PRO 2:1 My son, if you receive my words and treasure up my commandments with you,
PRO 2:2 make your ears pay attention to wisdom and incline your heart to understanding.
PRO 2:3 If you cry out for discernment and raise your voice for understanding,
PRO 2:4 if you seek it like you would seek silver and search for understanding as you would seek hidden treasures,
PRO 2:5 then you will understand the fear of Yahweh and you will find the knowledge of God.
PRO 2:6 For Yahweh gives wisdom; from his mouth comes knowledge and understanding.
PRO 2:7 He stores up sound wisdom for the upright; he is a shield for those who walk in integrity;
PRO 2:8 he guards the paths of justice and he will preserve the way of his faithful ones.
PRO 2:9 Then you will understand righteousness, justice, and equity, and every good path.
PRO 2:10 For wisdom will come into your heart, and knowledge will be pleasant to your soul.
PRO 2:11 Discretion will watch over you; understanding will guard you.
PRO 2:12 They will rescue you from the way of evil, from those who speak perverse things,
PRO 2:13 who abandon the paths of uprightness and walk in the ways of darkness.
PRO 2:14 They rejoice when they do evil and delight in the perversities of evil.
PRO 2:15 They follow crooked paths, and using deception they hide their tracks.
PRO 2:16 Wisdom and discretion will save you from an adulteress, from an immoral woman and her flattering words.
PRO 2:17 She abandons the companion of her youth and forgets the covenant of her God.
PRO 2:18 For her house sinks down to death, and her tracks will lead you to those in the grave.
PRO 2:19 All who go to her will not return again and they will not reach the paths of life.
PRO 2:20 So you will walk in the way of good people and follow the paths of righteous people.
PRO 2:21 For the upright will make a home in the land, and those with integrity will remain in it.
PRO 2:22 But the wicked will be cut off from the land, and the faithless will be cut off from it.
PRO 3:1 My son, do not forget my teaching, and keep my commandments in your heart,
PRO 3:2 for the length of your days and years of your life, they will add peace to you.
PRO 3:3 Do not let steadfast love and faithfulness ever leave you; tie them together about your neck; write them on the tablet of your heart.
PRO 3:4 Then you will find favor and a good reputation in the sight of God and man.
PRO 3:5 Trust in Yahweh with all your heart and do not lean on your own understanding;
PRO 3:6 in all your ways acknowledge him and he will make your paths straight.
PRO 3:7 Do not be wise in your own eyes; fear Yahweh and turn away from evil.
PRO 3:8 It will be healing to your flesh and refreshment for your bones.
PRO 3:9 Honor Yahweh with your wealth and with the firstfruits of all your produce,
PRO 3:10 and your storehouses will be filled up and your vats will be bursting, full of new wine.
PRO 3:11 My son, do not despise Yahweh's instruction and do not hate his rebuke,
PRO 3:12 for Yahweh disciplines those he loves, as a father deals with a son who pleases him.
PRO 3:13 The one who finds wisdom is blessed; he also gets understanding.
PRO 3:14 What you gain from wisdom is better than what silver will give in return and its profit is better than gold.
PRO 3:15 Wisdom is more precious than jewels and nothing you desire can compare to her.
PRO 3:16 She has the length of her days in her right hand; in her left hand are riches and honor.
PRO 3:17 Her ways are ways of pleasantness and all her paths are peace.
PRO 3:18 She is a tree of life to those who take hold of it; those who hold on to it are happy.
PRO 3:19 By wisdom Yahweh founded the earth; by understanding he established the heavens.
PRO 3:20 By his knowledge the depths broke open and the clouds dropped their dew.
PRO 3:21 My son, do not let these depart from your eyes: guard sound wisdom and discretion.
PRO 3:22 They will be life to your soul and an adornment of favor to wear around your neck.
PRO 3:23 Then you will walk on your way in safety and your foot will not stumble;
PRO 3:24 when you lie down, you will not be afraid; when you lie down, your sleep will be sweet.
PRO 3:25 Do not be afraid of sudden terror or devastation caused by the wicked, when it comes,
PRO 3:26 for Yahweh will be on your side and will keep your foot from being caught in a trap.
PRO 3:27 Do not withhold good from those who deserve it, when it is in your power to act.
PRO 3:28 Do not say to your neighbor, "Go, and come again, and tomorrow I will give it," when you have the money with you.
PRO 3:29 Do not make a plan to harm your neighbor— the one who dwells in safety with you.
PRO 3:30 Do not argue with a person without a reason, when he has done nothing to harm you.
PRO 3:31 Do not envy a violent person or choose any of his ways.
PRO 3:32 For the devious person is an abomination to Yahweh, but he brings the upright person into his confidence.
PRO 3:33 The curse of Yahweh is on the house of the wicked person, but he blesses the home of righteous people.
PRO 3:34 He mocks mockers, but he gives his favor to humble people.
PRO 3:35 Wise people inherit honor, but fools will be lifted up in their shame.
PRO 4:1 Listen, sons, to a father's instruction, and pay attention so you will know what understanding is.
PRO 4:2 I am giving you good instructions; do not abandon my teaching.
PRO 4:3 When I was a son of my father, the tender and only child of my mother,
PRO 4:4 he taught me and said to me, "Let your heart hold fast to my words; keep my commands and live.
PRO 4:5 Acquire wisdom and understanding; do not forget and do not reject the words of my mouth;
PRO 4:6 do not abandon wisdom and she will watch over you; love her and she will keep you safe.
PRO 4:7 Wisdom is the most important thing, so acquire wisdom and spend all you own so you can get understanding.
PRO 4:8 Cherish wisdom and she will exalt you; she will honor you when you embrace her.
PRO 4:9 She will put a wreath of honor on your head; she will give you a beautiful crown."
PRO 4:10 Listen, my son, and receive my words, and you will have many years in your life.
PRO 4:11 I am teaching you in the way of wisdom; I am leading you in upright paths.
PRO 4:12 When you walk, no one will stand in your way and if you run, you will not stumble.
PRO 4:13 Hold on to instruction, do not let it go; guard it, for it is your life.
PRO 4:14 Do not follow the path of the wicked and do not go along the way of those who do evil.
PRO 4:15 Avoid it, do not go on it; turn away from it and go another way.
PRO 4:16 For they cannot sleep until they do evil and they are robbed of sleep until they cause someone to stumble.
PRO 4:17 For they devour the bread of wickedness and drink the wine of violence.
PRO 4:18 But the path of righteous people is like the first light that grows brighter; it shines more and more until the fullness of the day comes.
PRO 4:19 The way of the wicked is like darkness— they do not know what it is they stumble over.
PRO 4:20 My son, pay attention to my words; incline your ear to my sayings.
PRO 4:21 Do not let them turn away from your eyes; keep them in your heart.
PRO 4:22 For my words are life to those who find them and health to their whole body.
PRO 4:23 Keep your heart safe and guard it with all diligence, for from it flow the springs of life.
PRO 4:24 Put crooked speech away from you and put corrupt talk far from you.
PRO 4:25 Let your eyes look straight ahead and fix your gaze straight before you.
PRO 4:26 Make a level path for your foot; then all your ways will be established.
PRO 4:27 Do not turn aside to the right or to the left; turn your foot away from evil.
PRO 5:1 My son, pay attention to my wisdom; incline your ears to my understanding,
PRO 5:2 so you may learn about discretion and your lips may protect knowledge.
PRO 5:3 For the lips of an adulteress drip with honey and her mouth is smoother than oil,
PRO 5:4 but in the end she is as bitter as wormwood, cutting like a sharp sword.
PRO 5:5 Her feet go down to death; her steps go all the way to Sheol.
PRO 5:6 She gives no thought to the path of life. Her footsteps wander; she does not know where she is going.
PRO 5:7 Now, my sons, listen to me; do not turn away from listening to the words of my mouth.
PRO 5:8 Keep your path far away from her and do not come near the door of her house.
PRO 5:9 In that way you will not give away your honor to others or years of your life to a cruel person;
PRO 5:10 strangers will not feast on your strength; your labor will not go into the house of foreigners.
PRO 5:11 At the end of your life you will groan when your flesh and your body waste away.
PRO 5:12 You will say, "How I hated instruction and my heart despised correction!
PRO 5:13 I would not listen to the voice of my teachers or incline my ear to my instructors.
PRO 5:14 I was almost completely ruined in the midst of the assembly, among the gathering of the people."
PRO 5:15 Drink water from your own cistern and drink running water from your own well.
PRO 5:16 Should your springs be dispersed everywhere and your streams of water flow in the public squares?
PRO 5:17 Let them be for yourself alone and not for strangers with you.
PRO 5:18 May your fountain be blessed and may you rejoice in the wife of your youth,
PRO 5:19 for she is a loving deer and a graceful doe. Let her breasts satisfy you at all times; may you be continually intoxicated by her love.
PRO 5:20 For why should you, my son, be captivated by an adulteress? Why should you embrace the bosom of an immoral woman?
PRO 5:21 Yahweh sees everything a person does and watches all the paths he takes.
PRO 5:22 A wicked person will be seized by his own iniquities; the cords of his sin will hold him tight.
PRO 5:23 He will die because he lacks instruction; he is led astray by his great foolishness.
PRO 6:1 My son, if you set aside your money as a guarantee for your neighbor's loan, if you gave your promise for a loan of someone you do not know,
PRO 6:2 then you have laid a trap for yourself by your promise and you have been caught by the words of your mouth.
PRO 6:3 When you are caught by your words, my son, do this and save yourself, since you have fallen into the hand of your neighbor; go and humble yourself and make your case before your neighbor.
PRO 6:4 Give your eyes no sleep and your eyelids no slumber.
PRO 6:5 Save yourself like a gazelle from the hand of the hunter, like a bird from the hand of the fowler.
PRO 6:6 Look at the ant, you lazy person, consider her ways, and be wise.
PRO 6:7 It has no commander, officer, or ruler,
PRO 6:8 yet it prepares its food in the summer and during the harvest it stores up what it will eat.
PRO 6:9 How long will you lie down, you lazy person? When will you rise from your sleep?
PRO 6:10 "A little sleep, a little slumber, a little folding of the hands to rest"—
PRO 6:11 and your poverty will come like a robber and your needs like an armed soldier.
PRO 6:12 A worthless person—a wicked man— lives by the crookedness of his speech,
PRO 6:13 winking his eyes, making signals with his feet and pointing with his fingers.
PRO 6:14 He plots evil with perversity in his heart; he always stirs up strife.
PRO 6:15 Therefore his disaster will overtake him in an instant; in a moment he will be broken beyond healing.
PRO 6:16 There are six things that Yahweh hates, seven that are an abomination to him:
PRO 6:17 the eyes of a proud person, a tongue that lies, hands that shed the blood of innocent people,
PRO 6:18 a heart that invents wicked schemes, feet that quickly run to do evil,
PRO 6:19 a witness who breathes out lies and one who sows discord among brothers.
PRO 6:20 My son, obey the command of your father and do not abandon the teaching of your mother.
PRO 6:21 Always bind them on your heart; tie them about your neck.
PRO 6:22 When you walk, they will guide you; when you lie down, they will watch over you; and when you wake up, they will teach you.
PRO 6:23 For the commands are a lamp, and the teaching is a light; the corrections that come by instruction are the way of life.
PRO 6:24 It keeps you from the evil woman, from the smooth tongue of an immoral woman.
PRO 6:25 Do not lust in your heart after her beauty and do not let her capture you with her eyelashes.
PRO 6:26 The price of a prostitute is the cost of a loaf of bread, but the wife of another man hunts for a precious life.
PRO 6:27 Can a man carry a fire against his chest without burning his clothes?
PRO 6:28 Can a man walk on hot coals without scorching his feet?
PRO 6:29 So is the man who goes to his neighbor's wife; the one who touches her will not go unpunished.
PRO 6:30 People do not despise a thief if he steals to satisfy his need when he is hungry.
PRO 6:31 Yet if he is caught, he will pay back seven times what he stole; he must give up everything of value in his house.
PRO 6:32 The one who commits adultery lacks sense; the one who does it destroys himself.
PRO 6:33 Wounds and shame are what he deserves and his disgrace will not be blotted out.
PRO 6:34 For jealousy makes a man furious; he will show no mercy on the day of vengeance.
PRO 6:35 He will accept no ransom and he cannot be bought off, though you offer him many gifts.

PRO 31:28 Her children rise up and call her blessed, and her husband praises her, saying,
PRO 31:29 "Many women have done well, but you surpassed them all."
PRO 31:30 Elegance is deceptive, beauty is vain, but a woman who fears Yahweh, she will be praised.
PRO 31:31 Give her the fruit of her hands and let her works praise her in the gates.
